# Agentic Alpha

## The Real Money in AI Agents (Spoiler: It's Not Building Agents)

Every founder wants to build the next "AI company." Nobody talks about building the boring thing that makes $40K a month.

This document maps where real money moves in the agent services economy right now. Not in 2030. In March 2026.

---

## The Market Landscape

**What's Already Proven**

Three service verticals have moved from theory to cash flow:

1. **AI Sales Development (AI SDR agencies)**
   - Services range: $10K–$50K/month
   - Delivery: Set up email sequences, web analytics, lead scoring, AI-assisted copywriting for a B2B company
   - Retention: 60–75% at 12 months
   - Scalability: High (one person can manage 8–12 clients)
   - Barrier to entry: Low (API access + copy-writing skill)

2. **Customer Support Automation (Chatbots + Knowledge Base)**
   - Build cost: $10K–$25K per deployment
   - Reusability: 80% of code transfers to next client (same platform, different data)
   - Client profile: E-commerce, SaaS, service businesses
   - Retention: 80%+ (switching costs are high once deployed)
   - Scalability: Medium (setup is custom per business, but deployment is 2–4 weeks)

3. **Vertical-Specific Automation (Industry-Specific Agents)**
   - Examples: Legal contract review agents, real estate valuation agents, HR interview screening
   - Revenue per client: $5K–$20K/month retainer
   - Build cost: 4–12 weeks (high upfront, high margin after)
   - Barrier: Domain expertise required (you have to understand the vertical)
   - Retention: 85%+ (embedded in core workflow)

**What's Overfunded and Crowded**

Agent infrastructure tools (CrewAI, LangGraph, LlamaIndex, etc.) are well-funded and well-engineered. Competing on tooling is hard. Competing on service delivery is easy.

Token launches (governance tokens for AI networks) sound hot but have sub-10% survival rate. Don't build here unless you have $5M+ and co-founder-tier domain expertise.

## The Money Paths (Ranked by Reality)

**Path #1: AI SDR Agency (Easiest Immediate Revenue)**

**What it is:** You manage AI-powered outbound campaigns for B2B companies. Sales leaders want more pipeline. You deliver it with AI-assisted copywriting + hybrid human QA + performance tracking.

**Revenue potential:** $10K–$50K/month, per client

**Time to first $10K:** 6–12 weeks

**Why it's the easiest:**
- No product to build (use existing platforms: email infrastructure, Claude API, web analytics)
- Repeatable process (same workflow for every client, different verticals)
- Measurable outcome (reply rate, meeting rate, pipeline value)
- Viable at small scale (one person can manage 5–10 clients)

**The workflow:**
1. Prospect research: Pull company data, past messaging, competitive landscape
2. Audience build: Source 500–2,000 target prospects via LinkedIn, Apollo, RocketReach
3. Message crafting: AI-assisted templates (Claude), human QA, tone matching
4. Sequence design: 5–7 touch sequence over 3–4 weeks
5. Deployment: Email infrastructure, landing pages, follow-up logic
6. Reporting: Weekly metrics (open rate, reply rate, booking rate, revenue pipeline)

**Pricing model:**
- Small clients ($1M–$10M revenue): $3K–$7K/month
- Mid-market ($10M–$100M revenue): $10K–$25K/month
- Enterprise ($100M+ revenue): $25K–$50K/month

**Unit economics:**
- Cost of service: ~$500/month (AI API, email infrastructure, your time allocation)
- Gross margin: 85%–95%
- Customer acquisition: Content marketing (blog, case studies), word-of-mouth
- Churn: 15–25% annually (normal for service businesses)

**How to win:**
- Specialise in one vertical (e.g., SaaS, fintech, real estate). Vertical expertise = 2–3x pricing power.
- Show results, not promises. Case study: "Generated $150K pipeline in 8 weeks, reply rate hit 18%."
- Automate your own process (use AI to help you write sequences, analyse data, optimise campaigns). If you don't dogfood your own stack, you're not ready to sell it.

**Path #2: Customer Support Automation (High Retention)**

**What it is:** Build AI-powered customer support chatbots + knowledge base systems for businesses. The chatbot handles 60–75% of inbound support tickets, humans handle escalations.

**Revenue potential:** $10K–$25K per deployment, $2K–$5K/month recurring

**Time to first project:** 4–8 weeks

**Why it's valuable:**
- Client saving: Usually $30K–$80K/year in support labour (even at 70% automation)
- Your build cost: $5K–$15K in labour (3–4 weeks of engineering)
- Your margin: 50–70% after 6 months (recurring revenue, minimal ongoing effort)
- Retention: 80%+ (switching costs are high; the bot is embedded in their helpdesk)

**The workflow:**
1. Audit existing support: What questions are asked? What's the ticket distribution?
2. Knowledge base build: Ingest documentation, FAQs, past ticket data
3. Chatbot setup: Deploy Claude API + conversation memory + escalation logic
4. Integration: Zendesk, Freshdesk, or Intercom connector
5. Testing & refinement: Run beta phase (2–3 weeks), tune responses
6. Handoff: Retrain the client to maintain + update the knowledge base

**Pricing model:**
- Build fee: $10K–$25K (one-time)
- Recurring: $2K–$5K/month (hosting, API costs, maintenance)
- Alternative: Revenue share (30–40% of support labour savings)

**Unit economics:**
- Build cost: ~$8K (3 weeks @ $50/hour equivalent engineer time)
- Recurring cost: ~$1K/month (hosting, API, monitoring)
- Gross margin: Build (60%), Recurring (80%)

**How to win:**
- Target businesses with 10–500 support tickets/month (sweet spot for automation ROI)
- Build a template product (same architecture, different knowledge bases). This lets you redeploy 80% of code.
- Show automation rate + ticket resolution time. "We automated 72% of tickets. Average resolution time dropped from 6 hours to 3 minutes."
- Manage the escalation: If you can't handle the hard cases (bugs, refunds, legal), the client loses trust. Build good escalation logic.

**Path #3: Vertical-Specific Agents (High Margin, Long Sales Cycle)**

**What it is:** Build an AI agent that automates a critical workflow in a specific industry. Examples: legal contract review, real estate appraisal, recruiting screening, insurance underwriting.

**Revenue potential:** $5K–$20K/month retainer per client, or $50K–$150K per deployment

**Time to first revenue:** 12–20 weeks

**Why it's harder but more valuable:**
- Switching cost is high (you're in their core workflow)
- Pricing power is high (you're solving a real bottleneck)
- Retention is extremely high (85%+)
- Sales cycle is longer (executives need to approve, risk-averse industries)
- Requires domain expertise (you have to understand the vertical deeply)

**Example: Legal Contract Review Agent**

**What it does:**
- Client uploads an NDA, service agreement, or lease
- Agent reads it, flags risk clauses, compares to standard templates, suggests revisions
- Junior attorney spends 30 minutes reviewing instead of 3 hours of paralegal + attorney time
- Result: Faster legal review, reduced risk of missed clauses

**Build cost:** 8–12 weeks (requires legal knowledge)
**Client willingness to pay:** $5K–$15K/month retainer
**Deployment time:** 4–6 weeks (testing, legal review, enterprise setup)

**Another example: Real Estate Appraisal Prediction Agent**

**What it does:**
- Agent ingests property data (square footage, location, comps, photos)
- Predicts fair market value within 5% margin
- Real estate agent saves 4 hours of research + appraisal ordering

**Build cost:** 6–8 weeks
**Client willingness to pay:** $3K–$8K/month
**Deployment:** 2–4 weeks

**How to win:**
- Pick a vertical you understand or can hire expertise in
- Talk to 10–20 practitioners before building (understand their exact workflow)
- Build for human+AI partnership, not full automation (courts require humans to sign off on legal advice)
- Sell to aggregators/platforms first (e.g., LawGeex, DocuSign) before selling to end clients (faster scale)

**Path #4: Agent Infrastructure Tools (VC-Scale, High Risk)**

**What it is:** Build open-source or commercial tools that make it easier to build agents. Examples: memory systems, tool calling orchestrators, evaluation frameworks.

**Revenue potential:** $0–$100K+/month (highly variable)

**Time to PMF:** 12–24 months

**The reality:** This is where 90% of VC money goes, where 80% of projects fail.

Infrastructure tools work only if:
1. You solve a real developer pain (existing tools are broken)
2. You build community (good OSS projects have 10K+ stars and active contributors)
3. You have a moat (hard to replicate, sticky to use, network effects)
4. You can monetise without destroying the open-source community

Most founder-founded infrastructure tools don't hit all four. Don't start here unless you have pre-existing expertise + capital.

**If you do build here:**
- Start open-source (free, on GitHub)
- Build observability/evaluation first (let developers see if their agents work)
- Don't charge for the tool itself (charge for hosting, managed service, enterprise features)
- Target developers first, not end companies

---

**Path #5: AI Token Launches (Low Effort, High Variance)**

**What it is:** Launch a token for an AI network (e.g., governance, compute, data).

**Revenue potential:** $0–$100M (if you luck into a bull market and hype cycle)

**Reality check:** 90% of AI token projects fail, most don't even launch. If you don't have deep crypto + AI expertise + already have a network, skip this.

## The Starter Stack (Under $50/Month)

If you're starting an agent service business, here's the minimum viable stack:

| Component | Tool | Cost |
|-----------|------|------|
| **Brain (LLM API)** | Claude API | $0–$100/mo (pay as you go) |

- Enterprise SaaS tools (too expensive at your scale)
- AI-specific infrastructure (use API-first tools, not self-hosted models)

## Key Principles

**1. Lead with working systems, not proposals.**

Don't pitch "I can build you an agent." Show them a working demo using dummy data. "Here's what your customer support bot would look like with your top 20 FAQs." Seeing is believing.

**2. Charge for outcomes, not hours.**

$10K flat fee for a customer support bot (outcome: 70% of support tickets automated) beats "$150/hour for agent development" (outcome: undefined).

Outcome-based pricing aligns incentives. You want it to work; they want it to work.

**3. Vertical specialisation beats horizontal tools.**

"AI agents" is too broad. "AI for legal discovery" is sellable. Vertical specialisation lets you:
- Use industry-specific data to train models
- Price 3–5x higher (vertical willingness to pay)
- Build credible case studies faster
- Reduce sales cycle (you speak the language)

**4. Reuse code, not clients.**

Build templated systems. First customer support bot takes 4 weeks. Second takes 1 week. Tenth takes 2–3 days.

If you're not reusing 70%+ of your code base per project, you're not scaling.

**5. Automate your own work first.**

If you're building AI agents for others but not using AI to automate your own workflow (sales, delivery, support), you don't understand your product.

Example: Use Claude to help you analyse client data, generate customer support transcripts to train your chatbot, or write project proposals.

**6. Measure and own the metric that matters.**

For SDR agencies: reply rate and pipeline value.
For support bots: ticket resolution rate and customer satisfaction.
For vertical agents: time saved per workflow + error reduction.

Pick one metric. Own it. Report it weekly.

**7. Build community if you scale.**

If Path 1 (SDR agency) or Path 2 (support bots) starts hitting $50K+/month, you'll want to:
- Build a community (Slack, Discord, public case studies)
- Publish results openly (transparency builds trust)
- License your IP to partners (someone else sells your playbook)
- Train others to deliver (hiring + standardisation)

This is how $50K/month agencies become $500K+/month platforms.

  → YES: Path #3 (Vertical-specific agent)
  → NO: Do you want to learn one?

## One-Year Revenue Scenarios

**Conservative (Path #1: AI SDR)**
- Months 1–3: Validate with 2 pilot clients (free/discounted)
- Months 4–6: Land 3–5 clients at $5K/month average = $15K/month
- Months 7–12: Grow to 8 clients, some upsell to $10K = $60K–$80K/month by year end

**Aggressive (Path #2: Support Bots)**
- Months 1–2: Build first bot (4 weeks delivery)
- Months 3–6: 2 more bots, recurring revenue starts ($6K/month)
- Months 7–12: 5 total deployments (recurring $12K/month + new builds $15K/month) = $27K/month by year end

**Long-term (Path #3: Vertical Agent)**
- Months 1–6: Research, build MVP, secure first client
- Months 7–12: Land 2 clients at $10K/month = $20K/month by year end
- Year 2: Scale to 5–8 clients = $60K–$80K/month

All three paths end at similar revenue ($50K–$100K/month), but with different time horizons and risks.

Pick the one that matches your strengths and patience.
