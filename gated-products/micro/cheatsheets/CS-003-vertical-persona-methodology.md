# Vertical Persona Methodology — Cheat Sheet

## What It Is

A method for reverse-engineering domain expertise into an agent persona. Instead of inventing a character, you extract one from how real practitioners think, talk, and assess risk.

## 5-Part Research Process

### 1. Extract Explicit Rules
- Industry regulations, compliance requirements, professional standards
- What practitioners *must* do (legal obligations, ethical codes)

### 2. Analyse Practitioners
- Study how experts in the field actually communicate
- Note vocabulary, sentence structure, formality level
- Identify what they explain vs. what they assume the audience knows

### 3. Map Knowledge Domains
- Core expertise (what they know cold)
- Adjacent knowledge (what they're aware of but would verify)
- Boundaries (what they'd refer to a specialist for)

### 4. Extract Communication Conventions
- How they structure advice (conclusion-first? evidence-first?)
- Jargon they use naturally vs. jargon they avoid
- How they handle uncertainty ("likely" vs. "possibly" vs. "I'd need to check")

### 5. Understand Risk Assessment
- What keeps practitioners in this field up at night?
- How do they weigh competing priorities?
- What's their default recommendation when uncertain? (conservative? aggressive?)

## Persona Document Template (6 Sections)

1. **Role & Expertise** — one sentence defining the persona's professional identity
2. **Knowledge Map** — core / adjacent / boundary domains in a table
3. **Communication Style** — vocabulary, structure, formality level
4. **Risk Profile** — conservative/moderate/aggressive + decision-making heuristics
5. **Boundaries** — what this persona explicitly does NOT advise on
6. **Sample Outputs** — 3 example responses showing the voice in action

## Quick Calibration

Write a response to a domain-specific question. Show it to someone in that field. If they say "this sounds like one of us," the persona works.

## Common Mistakes

- Making the persona too formal (real experts are often casual)
- Giving the persona perfect knowledge (real experts hedge and refer out)
- Ignoring risk tolerance (a startup lawyer ≠ a BigLaw lawyer)
