## Purpose

This template provides a structured data retention policy for organisations operating AI agent systems. It defines how long different categories of data are retained, the procedures for archival and deletion, and the regulatory requirements that drive retention decisions.

AI agent systems generate data types that traditional retention policies do not cover: conversation logs, embedding vectors, agent memory and context, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) indices, tool call records, and multi-agent orchestration traces. This policy template addresses these data types explicitly.

---

## 1. Policy Scope

This policy applies to all data created, collected, processed, stored, or transmitted by AI agent systems operated by the organisation, including:

- Data generated by agent interactions with users
- Data generated by agent-to-agent communication
- Data stored in agent memory, context windows, and knowledge bases
- Derivative data such as embedding vectors, summaries, and extracted entities
- Operational data such as logs, metrics, and audit trails
- Configuration data such as prompts, model parameters, and tool definitions

---

## 2. Data Classification Scheme

All data within scope must be classified according to the following scheme. Classification determines retention period, access controls, encryption requirements, and deletion procedures.

| Classification | Definition | Examples in Agent Systems | Handling Requirements |
|---------------|-----------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| **Restricted** | Highly sensitive data; unauthorised disclosure would cause severe harm | Encryption keys, authentication credentials, personally identifiable information (PII) in raw form | Encrypted at rest and in transit, access logged, strict need-to-know, secure deletion |
| **Confidential** | Sensitive business or user data; unauthorised disclosure would cause significant harm | User conversation content, agent system prompts, business logic in agent configurations | Encrypted at rest and in transit, access controlled, deletion verified |
| **Internal** | Data intended for internal use; not for external distribution | Agent performance metrics, internal tool call logs, development prompts | Access controlled, standard deletion procedures |
| **Public** | Data intended for or acceptable for public access | Published agent capabilities, public documentation, anonymised aggregate statistics | No special handling required, standard retention |

## 4. Auto-Purge Configuration

### 4.1 Purge Triggers

| Trigger Type | Description | Example |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **Time-based** | Data exceeds its retention period | Conversation logs older than 90 days |
| **Event-based** | A specific event triggers deletion | Account deletion triggers purge of all associated data |
| **Condition-based** | Data meets a defined condition | Temporary files with no access in 30 days |
| **Request-based** | Data subject requests deletion | GDPR Article 17 erasure request |
| **Policy-based** | Regulatory or business policy change shortens retention | New regulation requires shorter retention |

### 4.2 Purge Implementation Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Verification before purge | Confirm data is not subject to legal hold before deletion |
| Cascading deletion | When source data is purged, all derivatives must also be purged (embeddings, summaries, cached copies) |
| Deletion confirmation | Generate a deletion certificate: data type, volume, deletion timestamp, method |
| Backup purge | Purged data must also be removed from backups within 30 days |
| Audit record | The fact that data was deleted must be retained (in the audit log) even after data itself is purged |

### 4.3 Cascading Deletion Map

When a primary data type is deleted, the following derivatives must also be deleted:

| Primary Data Deleted | Cascade Deletions Required |
|---------------------|---------------------------|
| Conversation log | Embedding vectors derived from that conversation, agent memory referencing it, summaries, extracted entities |
| User account | All conversation logs, preferences, feedback, uploaded files, consent records |
| Agent configuration | Historical prompts (after retention period), associated test data |
| Training dataset | Derived model checkpoints (if model was sole consumer of that data) |

## 5. Legal Hold Procedures

A legal hold suspends all auto-purge and manual deletion for data relevant to anticipated or active litigation, regulatory investigation, or audit.

### 5.1 Legal Hold Workflow

| Step | Action | Responsible |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Legal counsel identifies the need for a hold | Legal |
| 2 | Hold notice issued: specifies data scope, custodians, and hold duration | Legal |
| 3 | Auto-purge rules suspended for in-scope data | IT / Data Engineering |
| 4 | Custodians acknowledge the hold | All identified custodians |
| 5 | Data preserved in immutable storage | IT / Data Engineering |
| 6 | Regular reminders sent to custodians | Legal |
| 7 | Hold released when matter concludes | Legal |
| 8 | Normal retention rules resume for previously held data | IT / Data Engineering |

## 6. Archival vs Deletion

| Approach | When to Use | Method | Access After Archival |
|----------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| **Deletion** | Data has no further legal, regulatory, or business value | Secure deletion (overwrite or crypto-shred) | Data is irrecoverable |
| **Archival** | Data has no operational value but must be retained for regulatory or legal reasons | Move to cold storage, encrypted, access-restricted | Available on request with approval |

### Archival Requirements

- Archived data must remain encrypted (AES-256 or equivalent)
- Access to archived data requires written justification and approval from data owner + legal
- Archived data must be included in legal hold scope when applicable
- Archival does not reset the retention clock — data archived at month 80 of a 7-year retention is deleted at month 84
- Archived data must be inventoried and the inventory reviewed annually

## 7. Regulatory Mapping

Different regulations impose different retention requirements. Where regulations conflict, retain for the longest required period.

| Regulation | Data Type | Minimum Retention | Maximum Retention | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------|
| GDPR (EU) | Personal data | No minimum | As short as necessary for purpose | Data minimisation principle; delete when no longer needed |
| GDPR Art. 17 | Personal data (on erasure request) | — | 30 days to process request | Must erase unless legal basis for continued processing |
| SOC2 | Audit logs, access logs | 1 year | — | Evidence for annual audit |
| PCI DSS | Cardholder data | — | As short as necessary | Do not store unless required; if stored, encrypt and limit retention |
| HIPAA (US) | Health information | 6 years | — | If agents process health data |
| CCPA (California) | Personal information | 12 months of request records | — | Must retain records of consumer requests for 24 months |
| SEC (US financial) | Communications | 3 years (general), 6 years (certain records) | — | If agents operate in financial services |
| Tax regulations | Financial records | 5-7 years (varies by jurisdiction) | — | Cost and billing data |

### Conflict Resolution

When two regulations impose conflicting requirements on the same data:

1. **Retain for the longest minimum period** required by any applicable regulation
2. **Do not exceed the shortest maximum period** unless doing so would violate another regulation
3. **Document the conflict** and the resolution in this policy
4. **Consult legal counsel** when regulations genuinely conflict

## 8. User Data Deletion Request Workflow (GDPR Article 17)

| Step | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|------|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Receive deletion request (email, in-app, support ticket) | Day 0 | Support / automated intake |
| 2 | Verify requester identity | Within 3 days | Support / Identity team |
| 3 | Determine if exceptions apply (legal obligation, public interest, legal claims) | Within 5 days | Legal / DPO |
| 4 | If no exceptions: initiate deletion across all systems | Within 10 days | Data Engineering |
| 5 | Cascade deletion to all derivative data (see Cascading Deletion Map) | Within 15 days | Data Engineering |
| 6 | Remove from backups (or flag for exclusion from backup restores) | Within 30 days | Infrastructure |
| 7 | Generate deletion certificate | Within 25 days | Data Engineering |
| 8 | Notify requester of completion | Within 30 days | Support / DPO |
| 9 | Retain record of the request and actions taken (not the deleted data) | 2 years | Compliance |

### Exception Grounds (Article 17(3))

Erasure may be refused if processing is necessary for:

- Exercise of freedom of expression and information
- Compliance with a legal obligation
- Public health purposes
- Archiving in the public interest, scientific/historical research, statistical purposes
- Establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims

Document the specific exception ground relied upon and notify the data subject.

## 9. Audit Requirements

| Audit Activity | Frequency | Scope | Output |
|---------------|-----------|-------|--------|
| Retention compliance check | Quarterly | Verify auto-purge is executing correctly; no data retained beyond policy limits | Compliance report with exceptions list |
| Deletion verification | On each deletion event | Confirm data and all derivatives are deleted | Deletion certificate |
| Legal hold audit | Quarterly | Verify all active holds are current and appropriate | Hold register review report |
| Policy review | Annually | Review retention periods against current regulatory requirements and business needs | Updated policy document |
| Backup retention audit | Bi-annually | Verify backups are rotated and purged data is not recoverable from backups | Backup audit report |
